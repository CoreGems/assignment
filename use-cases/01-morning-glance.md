# 01 — Morning glance

**Persona:** Alex Chen, regional sales rep, West Coast
**Setting:** Tuesday, 8:42 AM, in the car between client visits
**Surface:** PeakPath dashboard on a phone, one-handed

---

Alex pulls into a coffee shop parking lot before the 9 AM call. Phone out, three swipes, PeakPath is open.

What they see, top to bottom:

> *"Good morning, Alex."*

Below that, a **streak pill** glowing amber: *12-week streak above target*. To the right, a small **pulsing dot** next to a countdown: *Next payout in 4 days — May 25*.

The earnings card animates: counter ticks from $0 to **$48,320** in just over a second. A green chip slides in: *↑ 23% vs last year*. The monthly goal ring fills to **72%**, with **$8,640 / $12,000** in the middle. The sparkline beneath traces the year so far — a dip in March, a strong April recovery, May climbing.

Total time spent looking: **~9 seconds**. Alex puts the phone down, walks into the meeting, more confident than they were when they parked.

## Why this works

Sales reps don't have time to study a dashboard before every meeting. They need to feel something quickly — usually some flavor of *"am I doing OK?"* The PeakPath dashboard answers that in three places at once:

1. **Streak pill** — short-term momentum (am I on a roll?)
2. **Earnings counter + delta chip** — long-term trajectory (am I ahead of last year?)
3. **Goal ring** — current-period progress (am I on pace?)

Together, they answer the implicit question without a single click. Every value is also specific enough to be motivating: *"$48,320"* hits differently than *"earnings are up"*.

The entrance animation is intentional — money is emotional, and a number ticking up *feels* different from a number sitting still.

Three narrative walkthroughs of PeakPath in use, plus a note on the product thinking behind it. Each is a short read tied to specific UI moments in the prototype — try them against the [live demo](https://actress-seasons-arrived-incentive.trycloudflare.com).

| # | Scenario | Demonstrates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 01 | [Morning glance](./01-morning-glance.md) | At-a-glance dashboard, mobile usability, motivational tone |
| 02 | [Projection planning](./02-projection-planning.md) | Program detail page, **projection slider** (secondary signature interaction), tier crossing |
| 03 | [End-of-month review](./03-end-of-month-review.md) | 12-month trend sparkline, activity feed, achievement badges, narrative coherence |
| 04 | [Product thinking](./04-product-thinking.md) | Persona switch rationale, what this becomes at scale, intentional exclusions |

## Why these matter

A dashboard prototype is easy to evaluate as a *picture*. It's harder — and more useful — to evaluate as a *tool somebody uses on a Tuesday*. These scenarios walk through three real moments in a rep's week and show how the design earns its keep in each one. They also surface decisions that aren't obvious from screenshots alone (why glassmorphism only in dark, why no welcome modal, why the projection slider exists at all).

Read in order if you want the full arc; pick the one that interests you most if you're skimming.

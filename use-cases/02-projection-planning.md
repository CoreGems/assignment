# 02 — Projection planning

**Persona:** Maya Patel, regional sales rep, West Coast
**Setting:** Friday, 4:30 PM, laptop at desk, planning next week
**Surface:** PeakPath program detail page

---

Maya wants to know what to focus on Monday. Three programs are open. Two are coasting. One has real money on the table.

From the dashboard, she scans the **Active programs** row. The card marked *Featured* — **Q2 Enterprise Renewals** — shows a gradient outline and a Silver tier badge. *Silver → Gold* progress bar is ~26%. She taps in.

The detail page loads. Across the top, a horizontal **tier path**: Bronze ✓ — Silver ✓ — *(you are here)* — Gold ◯ — Platinum ◯. A pulsing gradient marker shows her current position, mid-Silver. Underneath each node, the dollar threshold: *$500 / $1,500 / $5,000 / $10,000*.

The **Projection card** sits to the left:

> *What if you close...*
>
> **$2,400** (projected payout)
>
> Additional deals: +0

She grabs the slider. Drags right.

- **+3 deals** → $3,540. *Still Silver.*
- **+5 deals** → $4,300. *Still Silver, but close.*
- **+7 deals** → $5,060. *Silver → Gold crossing.* The number pulses. A magenta line fades in below: *✨ Unlocks Gold tier.*

Gold pays out **$2,400 vs. Silver's $750** — a $1,650 jump.

Maya looks at the **6-week trend** chart and her **Milestones**: *20-deal milestone projected May 28, Gold tier unlocked projected June 4.* She has 40 days. She has the pipeline.

Monday morning, she knows exactly what to grind on.

## Why this works

The projection slider answers the **#1 question every sales rep has** but most incentive software doesn't:

> *"What do I need to do to earn more?"*

A static dashboard makes you do the math in your head. PeakPath does it live as you drag. The tier-crossing animation is the moment the design earns its keep — it's not just numbers updating, it's *unlocking* something.

This is also where the brand promise lands. *"Every sale is a step up"* — the tier path makes that literal, visually.
